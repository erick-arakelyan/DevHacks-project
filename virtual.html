Gandzasar Monastery

A 13th‑century Armenian cathedral located on a mountain—richly decorated and a regional spiritual center.

Shushi City

Historic hilltop city with cultural architecture, overlooking dramatic canyons.

Dadivank Monastery

Ancient monastic complex founded by disciple of Thaddeus the Apostle; rich with inscriptions & frescoes.

Grandmother and Grandfather Monument

Also called “We Are Our Mountains,” this stone monument near Stepanakert is a powerful symbol of Armenian heritage in Artsakh. Built in 1967, it represents the bond between the people and the land.

Tigranakert of Artsakh

Discover Tigranakert, an ancient city founded in the 1st century BCE by King Tigranes the Great. Located in Artsakh, it was a thriving Hellenistic city with classical urban planning, streets, and structures excavated by archaeologists. Tigranakert gives us a glimpse of the political, commercial, and cultural life in ancient Armenian history, standing as a testament to the region’s long heritage.

Amaras Monastery

Founded in the 4th century by Saint Gregory the Illuminator, Amaras Monastery is one of the oldest Christian institutions in the world. In the 5th century, Mesrop Mashtots, the inventor of the Armenian alphabet, opened a school here. Its walls have witnessed centuries of spiritual and cultural heritage.
